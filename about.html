About Ryan Branch

Hi! I'm Ryan Branch, a software engineer and scientific researcher from Michigan.

My hobbies include programming, 3D printing, electronics. I have a wide variety of software interests including simulations, computer vision, cryptography, smart contracts, procedural generation, and digital art.

Undergraduate Education

I attended the University of Michigan College of Engineering from September 2014 to April 2018.

During this time I participated heavily in scientific research, which you can read about here.

In April of 2018 I graduated with a B.S.E. in Chemical Engineering and a minor in Computer Science. Further educational details, including a list of coursework, are available here.

Employment

Most recently I have been a Product Engineer for Photon Semantics, a computer vision startup from Ann Arbor, MI which I co-founded in 2017 along with several other researchers and professors.
In this project I served as the technical lead and carried out a wide variety of R&D and engineering functions. Partnering with a global leader in the autonomous robotics industry, I developed both hardware and software for cutting-edge computer vision technologies with novel capabilities.
Prior to that, for two years I was an Automation & Controls Engineer at Eli Lilly, specializing in creation and deployment of computer systems for production monitoring and software control in pharmaceutical manufacturing environments.

My Resume page includes PDF downloads of both my résumé and cirriculum vitae, as well as information about professional certifications and entrepreneurial achievements.
